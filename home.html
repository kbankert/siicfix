What is SIIC?

The roots of our pain

stress

Recovery time is the problem

It's hard to control our natural fight-or-flight response, but we can control how we recover from it. The problem is that we are not giving ourselves enough recovery time. Stress turns against us when we go-go-go. When we stop thinking and start reacting. We grab for comfort foods and alcohol to cope. We snap at others and hate our imperfections. Symptoms include anxiety, chronic fatigue, stomach trouble, belly pain, insomnia, sleep interruptions, and muscle tightness.

The best recovery we can give ourselves is simply doing something we love to do. It's not restricted to meditation and yoga, and it's not going for a run if we don't like to run. Think about it: most of us don't experience symptoms until after college, which is exactly when we stop making time for the activities that we love to do.

Inflammation

It's like we're wearing a fat suit

Bloated is what we feel after we overeat Thai food. Inflamed is when we feel pregnant with a dead animal rotting in our stomach; when our joints ache for no reason. It's that daily headache we get at 4pm; our omega-6 rich American diet; our immune response when our weak digestive system lining percolates food into the bloodstream like a foriegn invader. Hopelessness can also inflame us, so when we continue to search and search for that miracle drug, we end up feeling worse, which only continues the downward spiral. Inflammation is not limited to our midsection; it affects our brain, joints, hips and every part of the body.

God Bless Turmeric, the all-natural anti-inflammatory. This is my quick-fix magic pill to reduce the bloat. The long fix is a difficult fix requiring a change in diet and a cleaned-up digestive system.

Immune deficiency

Our gut health is our health

Our immune system lies in our gut, so when we have digestive distress we are likely worn down catching colds and when we are worn down catching colds we are likely suffering from digestive distress. When our immune system is under the weather, it's difficult for us to combat foreign invaders, as well as home invaders, like food, stress, and Candida. Unfortunately, because these four culprits are so intertwined it is easy to get caught up in a downward spiral of stress, digestive distress, mental exhaustion and chronic fatigue. That said, have faith in knowing that the downward spiral can turn into a virtuous cycle.

The best medicine for our immune system is sleep and sun. Most of us are deficient in Vitamin D because we work office jobs, drive cars and wear sunscreen.

Candida overgrowth

Oh that Candida, she's the devil

Our microbiome houses trillions of bacteria and fungi, one of which is the fungus, Candida albicans. Candida loves to multiply, especially after we take a dose of antibiotics that kill off the bacteria that typically keep her in check. When Candida overgrows, she craves our attention, most notably in the form of a yeast infection, athlete's foot, jock itch and fungal nails. She feeds on wheat and sugar, so she makes us crave (like a crazy person) wheat and sugar. And, if our immune system is weak and gut lining impaired, she might seep into the bloodstream triggering an inflammatory response and symptoms such as headaches, brain fog, joint pain and depression. There are some antifungal quick-fixes to rid her, but she is one stubborn B and typically comes back in no time.

Strong-arm Candida by limiting grains, alcohol and sugar.
